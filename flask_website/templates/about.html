Predicting Clutch NBA Players

This website is the "fun" side of the project I did while at Galvanize. Here you can compare clutch stats of any two players who played between 2014-2018. Click on the 'Compare' tab and then put in the names of the players and click 'get stats'.

The goal of this project was to predict which player will be clucth in upcoming season. For this project, I got the play by play data for all the NBA games from last 14 years and looked at how all the players perform in the clutch situation. I described a game to be clutch if the score is within 6 points with only two minutes to play in regulation.

First, I got the data for only the last two minutes of the fourth quarter for all the games that were with in 6 points. After doing EDA and feature engineering, I gathered all the stats for each player in the clutch time. To see if there is a difference between the regular game and the clutch time I compared the stats with the regular game stats and found out players tend to be less efficient in the clutch time. Then I assigned different scores to different types of shots depending on level of difficulty. Then by looking at those scores, each player was given a clutch score which shows how good there are in clutch time and the score was used to determine if they are clutch or not.

For each year there were only 15-17% players that were clutch. From there I tried to predict which players will be clutch next year by looking at how they had performed in clutch situations in the previous years. I used Extreme Gradient Boosting (XGBoost) classifier and my final F1 score came out 14.13% higher than the baseline F1 score.
